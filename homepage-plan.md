# Homepage Plan

Prepared for: kefas4plateau.com  
Date: 2026-05-31  
Scope: One hero section plus four homepage sections.  
Content posture: factual, civic, source-backed, and non-persuasive.

## Homepage Strategy

The homepage should quickly establish:

- Who Chief Kefas Wungak Ropshik is
- His connection to Plateau State
- His documented public-service record
- The areas of work visitors can explore
- Where to find verified updates and contact information

The homepage should feel official, grounded, and human. It should avoid exaggerated promises or vote-seeking language.

## Visual Direction

Use a restrained PDP-inspired palette:

- White and off-white for most surfaces
- PDP green for identity, navigation, buttons, section markers
- PDP red as a thin accent only
- Dark ink text for readability
- Real photography wherever possible

Recommended image types:

- Professional portrait of Chief Kefas Wungak Ropshik
- Plateau landscape or civic/public engagement photo
- JUTH project photo
- Kefiano Creative Hub training photo
- Community outreach photo

Avoid:

- Generic political rally stock images
- Heavy gradients
- Oversized flag backgrounds
- Decorative abstract blobs
- Overuse of red
- Pill-shaped UI, decorative badges, and chip rows
- Eyebrow labels that repeat the heading or add no new information
- Repeated copy blocks that say the same thing in different words

## Simplicity Rules

- Every section should introduce a new reason to keep scrolling.
- Do not add a component unless it helps visitors choose where to go next.
- Prefer plain headings over eyebrow text.
- Prefer short, direct copy over repeated trust-building phrases.
- Use one primary action per section where possible.
- Avoid trust strips, decorative stat rows, and filler labels unless the data is verified and useful.

## Hero Section

### Goal

Introduce the public figure clearly and give visitors three immediate paths:

- Read his biography
- Explore documented projects
- View public issue themes

### Layout

Desktop:

- Full-width hero band
- Left side: text content
- Right side: real portrait or public-engagement photo
- Bottom edge should reveal the next section on first viewport
- No card around the hero text
- Optional thin PDP red line beside the heading area

Mobile:

- Image first or after headline, depending on photo quality
- Text left-aligned
- Buttons stacked or wrapped
- Keep hero height under full-screen so the next section peeks in

### Background

Preferred:

- Off-white background: `#F7FAF8`
- Subtle green side panel or image mask
- Thin red accent line, not a large red block

### Hero Content

Headline:

```text
Chief Kefas Wungak Ropshik
```

Subheadline:

```text
Popularly known as Kefiano, Chief Kefas Wungak Ropshik is a Plateau-born entrepreneur, philanthropist, and public figure associated with healthcare support, youth development, and community outreach.
```

Primary button:

```text
Explore Projects
```

Secondary button:

```text
Read Biography
```

Tertiary text link:

```text
View Latest Updates
```

### Hero Notes

Do not use:

- "Vote"
- "Join the movement"
- "The only choice"
- "A new dawn"
- "Rescue Plateau"
- Any direct electoral appeal

## Section 1: Public Service Snapshot

### Goal

Give visitors a fast, credible overview of the main areas of documented public work.

### Layout

Desktop:

- White background
- Section heading on left
- Four feature blocks in a 4-column grid
- Each feature block links to a relevant project/category page

Mobile:

- 2-column grid if space allows
- Otherwise stacked blocks

### Section Heading

```text
Public Service Snapshot
```

### Section Intro

```text
A quick view of documented areas of work associated with Chief Ropshik and Kefiano Global Foundation.
```

### Four Blocks

1. Healthcare
   - Copy: `Renovation and support connected to healthcare institutions, including the JUTH Accident and Emergency Unit.`
   - Link: `/public-service/healthcare`

2. Youth & Innovation
   - Copy: `Creative and digital skills training through initiatives such as Kefiano Creative Hub.`
   - Link: `/public-service/youth-innovation`

3. Community Support
   - Copy: `Reported outreach to widows, orphans, persons with disabilities, and vulnerable households.`

- Use unframed blocks with subtle dividers.

- Accent: small green icon circle with tiny red underline.

## Content Verification Notes

Before final build, get client confirmation for:

- Official portrait
- Approved biography
- Official project images
- Correct title and name spelling
- Whether the website should mention PDP directly in homepage body copy
- Final candidate/aspirant wording
- Official contact details

## Implementation Notes

- Keep homepage sections full-width, with constrained inner content.
- Do not place large cards inside larger cards.
- Do not add duplicate summary strips between sections.
- Avoid eyebrow text, pill rows, badges, and decorative metadata.
- Use stable image aspect ratios:
  - Hero image: `4 / 5` or `3 / 4`
  - Featured project image: `16 / 10`
  - Youth image grid: `1 / 1` and `4 / 3`
- Use buttons sparingly.
- Keep all section headings under two lines on mobile.
- Use real imagery as soon as the client provides it.
- Add skeleton or placeholder image slots during development, but do not expose placeholder labels in production.
